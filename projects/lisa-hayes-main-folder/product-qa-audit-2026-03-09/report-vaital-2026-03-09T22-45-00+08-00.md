## 4. The Enhancement List (Should Improve)

### Enhancement: Add a clearer public entry point or explain the root redirect
- **Area:** navigation / first-run experience
- **Observed friction:** Visiting `/` immediately redirects to `/login`. That may be intentional, but it removes all product context for first-time users unless they already know what VaiTAL is.
- **Why it matters:** New users lose orientation and trust when the app drops them straight into auth with no product explanation, security cues, or value proposition.
- **Recommendation:** Either keep a lightweight landing page at `/` or add a stronger context block on the login page explaining what VaiTAL does, who it is for, and why users should trust it with health data.
- **Expected benefit:** Better conversion, lower bounce, stronger first-impression trust.
- **Evidence:** No screenshot captured

### Enhancement: Improve the sign-up copy so it matches the screen state
- **Area:** sign-up UX / content clarity
- **Observed friction:** The sign-up screen headline changes to `Create Account`, but the supporting copy still says `Enter your credentials to access VaiTAL.` That line reads like login copy, not account-creation copy.
- **Why it matters:** Small wording mismatches make the product feel less polished and can create unnecessary hesitation during registration.
- **Recommendation:** Update sign-up helper copy to something explicit like `Create your VaiTAL account to start tracking labs and medications.`
- **Expected benefit:** Better clarity and a more trustworthy onboarding experience.
- **Evidence:** `/home/node/.openclaw/workspace/projects/lisa-hayes-main-folder/product-qa-audit-2026-03-09/screenshots/signup.png`

### Enhancement: Surface password requirements before submit
- **Area:** forms / validation
- **Observed friction:** The tested auth pages rely on browser-level validation for email format and required fields, but no visible password rules were presented before sign-up submission.
- **Why it matters:** Hidden password rules create rework and make the form feel arbitrary if the backend later rejects a password the UI never explained.
- **Recommendation:** Show inline password requirements near the field and confirm them in real time while typing.
- **Expected benefit:** Higher form completion, fewer failed submissions, lower support burden.
- **Evidence:** `/home/node/.openclaw/workspace/projects/lisa-hayes-main-folder/product-qa-audit-2026-03-09/screenshots/signup.png`

### Enhancement: Make auth-result messaging more explicit about next steps and timing
- **Area:** auth feedback / email flows
- **Observed friction:** Sign-up and forgot-password both show good confirmation states, but neither tested state exposed timing expectations, resend options, spam-folder guidance, or troubleshooting help.
- **Why it matters:** Email-based auth frequently fails at the delivery step, and users blame the product when they do not know what to do next.
- **Recommendation:** Add a short checklist after sign-up/reset: expected delivery time, spam/promotions note, resend link, and support fallback.
- **Expected benefit:** Better completion rate for email flows and fewer abandoned accounts.
- **Evidence:** `/home/node/.openclaw/workspace/projects/lisa-hayes-main-folder/product-qa-audit-2026-03-09/screenshots/signup-attempt.png`

## 5. Strategic Recommendations

1. **Complete an authenticated-path QA pass with a seeded test account**
   - Why it is high-impact: the real product value is almost certainly behind auth, and that area remains unverified.
   - Primary outcome: **retention** and **trust**

2. **Tighten onboarding and auth copy for first-time users**
   - Why it is high-impact: auth is the first trust gate for a health product, and clearer messaging can improve sign-up completion without engineering-heavy work.
   - Primary outcome: **conversion** and **trust**

3. **Add explicit email-flow recovery options**
   - Why it is high-impact: verification and reset flows are common abandonment points. A resend path and clearer next steps reduce user drop-off and support tickets.
   - Primary outcome: **conversion** and **support burden**
